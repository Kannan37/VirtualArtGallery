Michelangelo

(1475-1564)

Michelangelo was born on March 6, 1475, in Caprese (near Florence) in Tuscany. He was motherless by the age of six and fought long and hard with his father for permission to apprentice as an artist. Fashionable, but extremely jealous of Michelangelo's emerging talent.


The public first saw Michelangelo's frescoes on the vaulted ceiling of the Sistine Chapel on November 1, 1512, but some of those vaults you see are not real. The Renaissance artist spent four years painting the detailed Biblical scenes remembered by most people.

Michelangelo's Angel was produced in 1494-1495. The sculpture is made entirely from marble, and the piece measures 51.5 cm in height, including the base. The artwork is also sometimes known as Kneeling Angel or Angel with Candlestick.

The Pitti Tondo is an unfinished marble relief of the Virgin and Child by Michelangelo in round or tondo form. It was executed between 1503 and 1504 while he was residing in Florence and is now in the Museo nazionale del Bargello in Florence
